Insurance Agents Lose 60% of Quotes Before They Follow Up

April 01, 2026 4 min read By Call Boss Team

The Quote Graveyard

You ran the quote. You compared carriers. You put together a solid option — maybe even saved them $400 a year. You sent it over and... nothing. They didn't call back. Didn't email back. Didn't sign the application. So you called once. Left a voicemail. Maybe sent a follow-up email a few days later. Then a new quote request came in and you moved on. That quote you spent 20 minutes putting together? Dead. The prospect who was actively shopping for insurance? Went with someone else. Probably someone who called them twice while you called once. This is happening to insurance agents every single day. You're quoting 100 people a month and closing 40. The other 60? They evaporate. Not because your quote was bad. Not because the price was wrong. Because nobody followed up.

Why 60% Is Actually Conservative

Let's break down a typical month for an insurance agency: - 100 quote requests come in - 40 close within the first interaction or two (the easy ones) - 30 go silent after receiving the quote - 15 say "I need to think about it" (code for "someone else will call me first") - 10 say they're "shopping around" (code for "convince me you're worth it") - 5 explicitly say no (the only ones who are truly dead) That's 55 people who showed genuine buying intent — they literally asked for a quote — and dropped off because of insufficient follow-up. At a 45% recapture rate with proper follow-up, that's 25 more policies per month. If your average policy value is $1,500/year in premium, that's $37,500 in annual premium you're leaving on the table. Every single month.

The Insurance Follow-Up Dilemma

Insurance agents face a unique follow-up challenge: You're quoting all day. Running quotes takes time — comparing carriers, building proposals, customizing coverage. You spend your day creating value for prospects, which leaves almost no time to follow up with previous prospects. The CRM is a mess. Let's be honest — most agents' CRM looks like a junk drawer. Leads from 6 different sources, half-updated records, no consistent follow-up tasks, and a search function that makes you want to cry. There's no system. Follow-up happens when you remember, if you remember. There's no sequence, no cadence, no accountability. It's ad hoc at best. New quotes always win. A new inbound quote request will always take priority over following up with last week's quote. The new one feels more urgent. More exciting. More likely to close.

What AI Follow-Up Looks Like for Insurance

Here's what happens when every quote gets systematic AI follow-up: Immediately after sending the quote: - Text: "Hey [Name], just sent over your auto/home/life quote! Take a look when you get a chance. Any questions, just text me back — I'm here." - Email: Summary of the quote with a clear CTA Day 1 (4 hours later): - Text: "Did you get a chance to look at that quote? Happy to walk through it with you." Day 2: - Call from AI: "Hi [Name], just checking in on the quote we sent over. Any questions I can help with?" - If they express concerns, AI handles basic objections and escalates to agent if needed Day 3: - Text: "Quick question — are you comparing with other agencies? I want to make sure we're showing you the best option." Day 5: - Email with value-add: "3 Things Most People Miss When Comparing Insurance Quotes" Day 7: - Call: "Hey [Name], we'd love to earn your business. Is there anything we can adjust on that quote?" Day 14: - Text: "Still thinking about insurance? Our quote is valid for 30 days. Happy to update if anything's changed." Day 21: - Final push: "Last check-in — if you went another direction, no worries. But if you still need coverage, we're here." That's 8 touches over 21 days. Most agents do 1-2 touches total. The ones who do all 8 close 40-60% more business.

The Agent's Day — Before and After

Before AI: 6:00 AM — Alarm goes off. Check email. 4 new quote requests. 8:00 AM — Start quoting. First two are straightforward. 10:00 AM — Complex commercial quote. Takes 90 minutes. 11:30 AM — Remember you were supposed to follow up with 3 people from last week. Call one. Voicemail. Get distracted by inbound call. 12:00 PM — Lunch. 1:00 PM — More quoting. Two more come in via website. 3:00 PM — Try to follow up again. One person answers, asks a question, says they'll call back. They won't. 4:00 PM — Return calls from existing clients. Policy change requests. 5:00 PM — Go home knowing you didn't follow up with half the people you should have. After AI: 6:00 AM — Check dashboard. AI booked 3 appointments yesterday from follow-ups. 8:00 AM — Start quoting. AI is handling all follow-up from previous days automatically. 10:00 AM — Client from AI follow-up calls in: "I got your texts, I'm ready to move forward." 11:00 AM — Keep quoting. AI is texting, calling, and emailing on your behalf. 2:00 PM — Dashboard shows 2 more appointments booked. One is a quote from 2 weeks ago that finally responded. 4:00 PM — Finish the day knowing every single quote you sent has active follow-up running. Same hours. Same effort. 40-60% more closings.

Stop Losing the Quotes You Already Won

Here's the frustrating truth: you already did the hard work. The prospect found you. They gave you their information. You ran the quote. You created a competitive option. All that's left is following up. And that's where 60% of your business dies. AI doesn't let quotes die. It follows up relentlessly, intelligently, and personally — on every single quote, every single time. Your quotes aren't losing to better prices. They're losing to better follow-up. Fix that.
