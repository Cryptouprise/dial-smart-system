TCPA Compliance for AI Dialers: What You Need to Know

January 30, 2026 8 min read By Call Boss Team

TCPA Basics: What Every Dialer User Must Know

The Telephone Consumer Protection Act (TCPA) regulates automated calling. Key rules: no calls before 8am or after 9pm (local time), honor the National DNC Registry, maintain your own internal DNC list, get prior express written consent for marketing calls, and provide opt-out mechanisms on every call.

AI Dialers and TCPA: Special Considerations

AI voice agents add new compliance dimensions. You must disclose that the caller is AI (some states require this), ensure AI responses don't make unauthorized commitments, log all conversations for compliance audits, and implement real-time DNC checking before every call.

Penalties for Non-Compliance: Why This Matters

TCPA violations carry penalties of $500-$1,500 per call. For a campaign of 1,000 calls, a single compliance violation could result in $500K-$1.5M in fines. This isn't theoretical — TCPA lawsuits are the most common consumer complaint filed with the FCC.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are AI dialers legal under TCPA?
Yes, AI dialers are legal when used in compliance with TCPA regulations. This includes following calling hour restrictions, honoring DNC requests, obtaining proper consent, and providing opt-out mechanisms.
What are the penalties for TCPA violations?
TCPA violations can result in fines of $500 per call for standard violations and $1,500 per call for willful violations. Class action lawsuits can reach millions of dollars.
