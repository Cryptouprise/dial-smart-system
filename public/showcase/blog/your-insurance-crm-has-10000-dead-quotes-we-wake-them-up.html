Your Insurance CRM Has 10,000 Dead Quotes. We Wake Them Up.

April 01, 2026 4 min read By Call Boss Team

The Quote Graveyard Revisited

Every insurance agent knows the feeling. You open your AMS or CRM and there they are — thousands of quotes that went nowhere. Names, addresses, coverage details, premium comparisons. All that work. All that data. All sitting there like tombstones in a graveyard. 10,000 dead quotes isn't unusual for an agency that's been operating for 5-10 years. Some agencies have 20,000. 30,000. Each one represents: - 15-30 minutes of agent time (quoting, comparing, building proposals) - A prospect who was actively shopping for insurance - An opportunity that was THIS close to being a client And what happened? Agent followed up once. Maybe twice. Prospect went quiet. Agent moved on. Quote died.

Why Dead Quotes Aren't Really Dead

Here's the thing about insurance quotes: the need never goes away. A solar lead might decide they don't want panels. A car buyer might decide to keep their old car. But an insurance prospect? They NEED insurance. They didn't stop needing it — they just bought it from someone else. But "someone else" might not be working out: - Their rate went up at renewal - Claims service was terrible - They never hear from their agent - They got a surcharge they didn't expect - They realized they're underinsured Every one of your dead quotes is a person who is currently insured — and potentially unhappy. They already showed purchase intent with you. They already shared their information. They're pre-qualified. All you have to do is ask: "Hey, happy with your current coverage?"

The AI Quote Resurrection Campaign

Step 1: Segment by recency and type - 0-6 months old: High priority. They recently shopped and might not have locked in yet. - 6-12 months old: Medium priority. First renewal coming up — if they're unhappy, now's the time. - 12-24 months old: They've been through one renewal cycle. If rates went up, they're ripe. - 24+ months old: Lower conversion but still worth contacting. Update contact info, check if they still need the same coverage. Segment by line of business too: - Auto quotes → "Rates drop after 6 months of clean driving. Want me to check?" - Home quotes → "With home values changing, your coverage needs might have shifted." - Life quotes → "Life insurance rates go up with age. The quote from [year] would be more expensive today." Step 2: Personalized, contextual outreach AI reaches out with messaging that references the original interaction: "Hi [Name], this is [Agency]. We put together an insurance quote for you back in [Month/Year]. A lot has changed in the market since then, and I'm seeing some really competitive rates. Want me to run updated numbers? No obligation — takes 5 minutes." This is miles more effective than a generic marketing blast because: - It's personalized (their name, the timing) - It references a real interaction (you quoted them) - It acknowledges time has passed (things have changed) - It's low-pressure (no obligation, 5 minutes) Step 3: Multi-touch over 30 days Same 8-12 touch playbook. Real Results

An independent agency with 8,500 dead quotes ran an AI reactivation campaign: - Response rate: 9.3% (792 conversations) - Requote requests: 38% of conversations (301 requotes) - Bind rate from requotes: 34% (102 new policies) - Average annual premium: $2,400 - New annual premium generated: $244,800 - Campaign cost: ~$1,200 $1,200 → $244,800 in annual premium. That's a 204X return. And the lifetime value (5-7 year retention average): $1.2-1.7 million in total premium from a $1,200 campaign.

Make It Recurring

Don't run this once. Run it quarterly: - Q1: Reactivate Q4 and older quotes (catch renewal shoppers) - Q2: Reactivate Q1 quotes + target spring homebuyers - Q3: Reactivate Q2 quotes + commercial renewal prep - Q4: Reactivate Q3 quotes + Open Enrollment push Every quarter, the database is swept. Every dead quote gets another chance. The pipeline is always full without buying a single new lead.

10,000 Quotes. 10,000 Opportunities.

Your dead quotes aren't dead. They're dormant. They're people who need insurance, already know your agency, and are waiting for someone to ask if they're happy with what they have. AI asks that question — systematically, persistently, and at scale. Wake up the quotes. Wake up the revenue.
