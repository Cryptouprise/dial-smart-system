Canvassers vs AI: The Real Cost Comparison

April 01, 2026 4 min read By Call Boss Team

The Canvasser Model Is Breaking

Door-to-door canvassing has been the backbone of residential solar sales for a decade. Send an army of twenty-somethings into neighborhoods, knock on doors, set appointments. It works. Or at least, it used to work. Here's the reality of canvassing in 2026: - Turnover is 60-80% annually. You're basically running a training academy that graduates people into your competitor's company. - No-show rates from canvassed leads are 40-50%. People say yes to the young, enthusiastic person on their doorstep and then hide when the closer shows up Saturday. - It only works in good weather. Rain, extreme heat, snow — your setters stay home and your pipeline dies. - It's geographically limited. You can only canvass areas your team can physically reach. - It's getting harder. No soliciting signs. Ring cameras. Noise ordinances. HOA restrictions. People are less willing to open their doors than ever. - The economics are brutal. When you add up salary, gas, vehicle wear, training time, and management overhead, a canvassed appointment costs more than most people realize. I'm not saying canvassing is dead. I'm saying the ROI is declining rapidly, and there's a better way to spend those dollars.

The Real Cost of a Canvasser

Let's break it down honestly: Direct costs per canvasser: - Base pay: $2,500-3,500/month (or $15-20/hour) - Commissions per appointment: $50-100 - Gas/transportation: $300-500/month - Phone: $50-100/month - Insurance/workers comp: $200-400/month - Total per canvasser: $3,500-5,000/month Indirect costs: - Manager (1 per 6-8 canvassers): $5,000-7,000/month, divided = $750-1,000/canvasser - Training costs (2-3 weeks before they're productive): $1,500-2,500 per new hire - Recruiting costs (constant, given 60-80% turnover): $500-1,000/month/canvasser - Vehicle/insurance if company-provided: $800-1,200/month - Total indirect: $1,500-3,000/month per canvasser Total all-in cost per canvasser: $5,000-8,000/month Average output per canvasser: - 15-25 appointments per month (good performers) - No-show rate: 40-50% - Net kept appointments: 8-15 per month Cost per kept appointment from canvassing: $400-1,000 That's not a typo. When you factor everything in, a canvassed appointment that actually shows up costs $400-1,000.

The Real Cost of an AI Appointment

Now let's look at AI: AI system costs: - Platform fee: $3,000-8,000/month (depends on volume) - Telecom costs: $0.02-0.05 per call/text - CRM integration: typically included - No gas, no vehicles, no turnover, no training, no sick days Output: - 50+ appointments per day possible (1,000+/month at scale) - No-show rate with AI confirmation: 12-18% - Net kept appointments: 820-880 per month (at 1,000 booked) Cost per kept appointment from AI: $3-10 Read that again. $3-10 per kept appointment vs. $400-1,000 per kept appointment.

"But the Quality Is Different"

This is the objection I always get. "Canvassed leads are higher quality because there was a face-to-face interaction." Let's examine that: Canvassed lead quality: - Pro: Face-to-face rapport - Con: 40-50% no-show rate (how much rapport was there really?) - Con: Many appointments are set just to get the canvasser off the porch - Con: Spouse often wasn't present and wasn't consulted - Con: Quality depends entirely on the individual canvasser AI-set appointment quality: - Pro: 12-18% no-show rate (lead confirmed multiple times) - Pro: Both homeowners were qualified - Pro: Electric bill and homeownership verified - Pro: Lead received information and had time to discuss with spouse - Con: No face-to-face pre-sell Look at those no-show rates. Canvassing: 40-50%. AI: 12-18%. Which one actually has higher quality leads in the closer's calendar?

The Hybrid Model

Smart solar companies are going hybrid. Keep your best 3-4 canvassers for high-value neighborhoods. Let AI handle everything else. Here's what that looks like: AI handles: - All inbound lead response (speed to lead) - All follow-up for every channel - All database reactivation - All no-show recovery - All appointment confirmation - Outbound calling to new lists Canvassers handle: - Premium neighborhoods with high home values - Areas where face-to-face is a clear advantage - Events, home shows, community fairs You get the best of both worlds: AI's scale and consistency for 90% of your pipeline, and human touch for the high-value 10%.

The Canvasser Transition

If you've got 15 canvassers and you're considering AI, here's how to think about it: - 15 canvassers at $6,500/month all-in = $97,500/month - Average output: 300 appointments, 165 kept (55% show rate) - Cost per kept appointment: $591 Replace 10 canvassers with AI: - AI cost: ~$6,000/month - 5 remaining canvassers: $32,500/month - AI output: 800+ appointments/month, 680 kept (85% show rate) - Canvasser output: 100 appointments, 55 kept - Total kept appointments: 735 - Total cost: $38,500 - Cost per kept appointment: $52 You went from 165 kept appointments for $97,500 to 735 kept appointments for $38,500. That's 4.5X the appointments for less than half the cost.

Make the Switch

Canvassing was the best tool available for a long time. It's not anymore. The math has fundamentally shifted, and the companies that recognize this first will dominate their markets. Your canvassers can't knock on 10,000 doors a day. AI can call 10,000 people a day. The door-to-door era is ending. The AI era has begun.
