19. "It's Too Expensive" — The Math on a Missed Lead

April 01, 2026 3 min read By Call Boss Team

Overview

Let's do math. I know attorneys love arguing, so argue with the numbers. The Cost of a Lead What do you spend on marketing? Let's say $10,000 per month on Google Ads for personal injury leads. You get 100 leads. That's $100 per lead. How many convert to clients? Industry average for law firms is 20-30%. Let's be generous and say 25%. That's 25 clients per month. What happened to the other 75 leads? They called and got voicemail. They filled out a form at 9 PM. They didn't get a callback fast enough. They got a generic auto-response and called someone else. They needed follow-up and nobody followed up. You paid $7,500 for those 75 leads and got nothing. The Cost of AI Our system costs a fraction of a single employee's salary. For the sake of argument, let's say it's a few thousand dollars per month. Now let's say it improves your conversion rate from 25% to 35%. That's not aggressive — that's what happens when you respond in 5 seconds and follow up 15 times instead of twice. 35% of 100 leads = 35 clients. That's 10 additional clients per month. The Revenue of Those 10 Clients Personal injury: 10 cases x $50,000 average fee = $500,000 Family law: 10 cases x $5,000 average retainer = $50,000 Criminal defense: 10 cases x $5,000 average retainer = $50,000 Bankruptcy: 10 cases x $3,000 average fee = $30,000 Whatever your practice area, 10 extra clients per month is real money.
