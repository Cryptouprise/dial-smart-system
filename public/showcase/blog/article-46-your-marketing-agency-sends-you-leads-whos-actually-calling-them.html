Article 46: Your Marketing Agency Sends You Leads. Who's Actually Calling Them?

April 01, 2026 4 min read By Call Boss Team

Overview

You're paying your marketing agency $5,000, $10,000, maybe $20,000 a month. They're running Google Ads, managing your LSAs, doing SEO, maybe some social media. And they send you reports that look great: 150 leads this month! Cost per lead is down! Impressions are up! But here's the question nobody's asking: who's calling those 150 leads?

The Broken Handoff

Marketing agencies generate leads. That's their job. They put your firm in front of people searching for legal help, get them to click, and capture their information. But the agency's job ends when the lead hits your CRM. From there, it's on you. And "on you" usually means: - Your intake coordinator sees the lead notification (eventually) - They call when they get around to it (20 minutes to 4 hours later) - If the lead doesn't answer, they leave a voicemail - Maybe they follow up once or twice - The lead either answers or it doesn't - On to the next one Your agency delivered the lead. You fumbled the follow-up. The lead signed with another firm. And when you look at the numbers, you blame the agency for "bad leads." Sound familiar?

The Data Your Agency Doesn't Show You

Your marketing agency shows you lead volume, cost per lead, and maybe click-through rates. What they don't show you — because they can't — is: - How long it took your team to respond to each lead - How many leads were never contacted at all - How many leads were contacted once and then abandoned - How many leads went to voicemail and never got a callback If your agency could show you that data, the conversation would change very quickly. Because the problem isn't lead quality. The problem is lead handling.

The Speed Gap Is Costing You Fortune

Here's a scenario that plays out every single day: Your agency generates a lead at 2:47pm. The prospect just searched "personal injury lawyer near me," clicked your ad, and filled out your form. They're motivated. They're ready. Your intake coordinator is on another call. She sees the notification at 3:15pm — 28 minutes later. She calls. Voicemail. She leaves a message and moves on. The prospect, who filled out four contact forms at 2:47pm, answered the first firm that called at 2:49pm. They had a great conversation. They booked a consultation. By the time your team called at 3:15pm, the prospect was already committed to someone else. Your agency did their job perfectly. Your intake process failed.

AI Closes the Gap

When a lead hits your system from any source — Google Ads, LSAs, website forms, referrals, social media — AI responds within 5 seconds. Every time. Every source. - Call initiated (AI voice agent or immediate human connection) - Text message sent (with a personalized greeting) - Email delivered (with firm info and CTA) All three happen simultaneously, within 5 seconds of form submission. No delay. No waiting. No "I'll get to it after this call." And if the lead doesn't answer the first attempt? AI follows up. 8, 10, 12 times across multiple channels over the next 14 days. Not a single lead falls through the cracks.

Making Your Marketing Investment Actually Work

Here's the real talk: if you're spending $15,000/month on marketing and your team is only properly handling 60% of leads, you're effectively flushing $6,000/month down the toilet. Adding AI intake doesn't replace your marketing agency. It makes your marketing agency's work actually matter. Every dollar you spend on lead generation becomes more valuable because every lead gets handled properly. Firms that add AI intake on top of their existing marketing typically see: - 30-50% increase in leads converted to consultations - 25-40% decrease in cost per signed client - ROI on marketing spend jumps from 2-3X to 5-8X That $15,000/month marketing spend that was generating $45,000 in cases? With AI intake, it generates $75,000-$120,000. Same agency. Same ads. Same budget. Just better follow-through.

The Accountability Layer

Here's a bonus: AI creates complete data on every lead interaction. Response time, number of attempts, channels used, conversation content, disposition. Now when your agency asks "how are the leads converting?" you have real data. And when the answer is "95% contacted within 5 seconds, 12-touch follow-up on every lead, 35% consultation rate" — that's a conversation about optimization, not blame.
