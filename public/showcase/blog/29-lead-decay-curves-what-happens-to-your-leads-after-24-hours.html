29. Lead Decay Curves: What Happens to Your Leads After 24 Hours

April 01, 2026 3 min read By Call Boss Team

Overview

I want to show you what happens to a lead's likelihood of conversion as time passes. It's not a slow decline. It's a cliff. The Decay Timeline: - 0-5 minutes: Lead is hot. They're actively looking. Conversion potential is at maximum. - 5-30 minutes: Lead is cooling. They may have contacted other firms. Conversion drops 50%. - 30-60 minutes: Lead is lukewarm. The emotional impulse is fading. Conversion drops 75%. - 1-24 hours: Lead is cold. They've either hired someone else or the urgency has passed. Conversion drops 90%. - 24-72 hours: Lead is nearly dead. You'd need significant follow-up to revive. Conversion drops 95%. - 72+ hours: Lead is ice cold. They've forgotten they called you. The matter may have resolved itself, been handled by another firm, or been abandoned entirely. The Math of Decay If 100% of your leads have "conversion potential" at minute zero, and 80% of that potential evaporates within 5 minutes of no response, your effective lead pool is 20% of what you paid for within minutes of acquisition. You spent $100 per lead. By the time you call back tomorrow, that lead is worth $5. Why Legal Leads Decay Faster Legal leads decay faster than most industries because: 1. High emotional stakes. The person calling was in an emotional state. Emotion drives action. When the emotion fades, so does the action. 2. Multiple firm contact. 78% of legal leads contact more than one firm.
