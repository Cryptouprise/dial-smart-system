Debt Is Not an Impulse Purchase

April 01, 2026 4 min read By Call Boss Team

Overview

In most sales, there's a decision window. Someone shops for a car for 2-3 weeks. They compare software products for a few days. They choose a restaurant in 30 seconds. Debt settlement is not like that. At all. A person with $50,000 in credit card debt didn't accumulate it in a day. They've been living with it for years. They've been ignoring it, making minimums, robbing Peter to pay Paul. The decision to actually DO something about it -- to enroll in a program that requires admitting the problem is too big to handle alone -- takes time. Lots of time. Studies and our own data show that the average debt settlement enrollment happens 47-90 days after first contact. Not first contact to enrollment same-day. Forty-seven to ninety DAYS. That means when you buy a lead today, you shouldn't expect them to enroll today. Or this week. Or probably even this month. You should expect to be in consistent, valuable contact with them for 2-3 months before they're ready.

Why 30-60 Touches?

It sounds aggressive. 30-60 touches over 90 days? Won't they get annoyed? No. Because: 1. Not every touch is a phone call. Touches include texts, emails, voicemails, content shares, reminders. The mix matters. 2. Debt leads expect persistence. They know they need help. They WANT someone to keep showing up. They just need time. 3. Each touch serves a purpose. Early touches educate. Middle touches build trust. Late touches create urgency. It's not 60 versions of "ready to enroll yet?" 4. Life provides natural trigger points. Monthly statements. Collection calls. Credit score alerts. Tax season. Holidays. Each one creates a moment where the lead's pain spikes and they're more receptive.

What 90 Days of AI Nurture Looks Like

Week 1: Aggressive initial contact - Day 0: Call + text + email within 5 seconds - Days 1-3: Daily call attempts + texts - Days 4-7: Alternate calls and texts, introduce educational content Weeks 2-4: Education and trust building - 2-3 touches per week - Content: debt management tips, program explanations, FAQ answers - Personalized based on their specific debt type and amount - Text conversations that answer questions in real time Month 2: Deepening engagement - Weekly touchpoints - Success stories and testimonials (anonymized) - Cost-of-waiting content: "Your $50,000 in debt is accumulating $X per month in interest" - Qualification check-ins: "Anything changed in your situation?" Month 3: Conversion push - Increased frequency to 2-3 touches per week - Direct enrollment invitations - Urgency messaging: "Programs like this have limited enrollment windows" - Deadline-based CTAs Post-90 days: Maintenance - Monthly check-ins indefinitely - Major life event triggers (new year, tax season, back-to-school spending) - "Still here when you're ready" positioning

Why Humans Can't Do This

A human sales rep managing a pipeline of 200 leads at various stages of a 90-day nurture cycle needs to: - Track where each lead is in the sequence - Customize messaging based on their specific situation and previous conversations - Hit the right touchpoints on the right days - Handle responses in real time - NOT miss any scheduled follow-ups - Also work new incoming leads This is literally impossible for a human to do well. Something always slips. The 3-week-old lead gets forgotten because the 3-day-old lead responded. The carefully planned Day 47 re-engagement text never gets sent because the rep is on PTO. AI does all of this simultaneously for every lead. Thousands of leads, each on their own personalized 90-day journey, each getting the right message at the right time. No slippage. No forgotten touchpoints. No missed windows.

The Enrollment Curve

Here's what the enrollment timeline looks like with proper 90-day nurture: - Week 1: 8% of enrollments (the ready-now people) - Weeks 2-4: 22% of enrollments (quickly warmed) - Month 2: 35% of enrollments (the education-converted) - Month 3: 25% of enrollments (the slow-burn) - Post-90 days: 10% of enrollments (the eventual converts) If you only follow up for 1-2 weeks (like most teams do), you're capturing the first 30% and losing the other 70%. Seventy percent of your enrollments come after the point where most sales teams give up. That's the 90-day nurture in one stat. The longer you stay in the game, the more you win.
