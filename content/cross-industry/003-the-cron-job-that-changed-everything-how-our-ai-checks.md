## What Is a Cron Job (And Why Should You Care)?

A cron job is a scheduled task that runs automatically at set intervals. That's it. It's not new technology. It's not revolutionary computer science. Your email server probably runs dozens of cron jobs right now.

But here's what makes ours different: every five minutes, our system examines every single lead in your entire CRM. Not the new ones. Not the "hot" ones. Not the ones a rep flagged for follow-up. Every. Single. One.

It reads every conversation transcript. It checks every disposition status. It evaluates every scheduled task. It reviews the full history of interactions. And then it decides — in real time — what needs to happen next for each lead, and it does it.

That's not a drip campaign. That's not a workflow. That's a system that never stops thinking about your pipeline.

## Why Every Five Minutes Matters

Let me put this in perspective with some math.

There are 1,440 minutes in a day. Our system checks your entire pipeline 288 times per day. That's 288 opportunities to catch a lead that fell through a crack. 288 chances to notice that someone responded to a text but nobody followed up. 288 moments where the system says "this lead needs attention right now" and gives it attention right now.

Your best sales rep checks their pipeline maybe 3-4 times per day. Maybe. Between bathroom breaks, lunch, the lead they're currently on the phone with, and — let's be honest — whatever else they're doing on their phone.

288 vs. 4. That's the gap.

**12:00:15 PM — Cycle complete**

Total time: 15 seconds. 5,000 leads evaluated. Hundreds of actions taken. Zero human involvement.

**12:05:00 PM — It starts again.**

## The Problems This Solves (That You Didn't Know You Had)

### The "Fell Through the Cracks" Problem
Every sales manager says it: "No lead falls through the cracks on my team." Every sales manager is lying. Leads fall through cracks constantly. A rep gets sick. A rep quits. A rep gets busy and forgets. A lead gets miscategorized. A follow-up gets scheduled but never executed.

When a system checks every lead every five minutes, there are no cracks. There's nowhere for a lead to hide. There's no lead sitting in a pipeline stage for 72 hours with nobody noticing.

### The "Response Time" Problem
A lead fills out a form on your website at 2:47 AM. Your team gets in at 9:00 AM. That lead has been sitting for over 6 hours. Worse — they probably filled out your competitor's form at 2:49 AM and got a call at 2:50 AM.

Our cron job catches that lead at 2:50 AM. Call, text, email. Five seconds.

### The "Stale Pipeline" Problem
You've got 30,000 leads in your CRM. How many of them are being actively worked? Be honest. Probably 500-1,000. The rest are "dead leads" that nobody's touching. Except they're not dead. They're stale. Big difference.

Our system doesn't have stale leads because it looks at every lead every five minutes. That lead from 6 months ago who said "not right now"? The system knows it's been 6 months. It sends a re-engagement: "Hey, it's been a while — just checking in to see if anything's changed on your end." That lead might be ready now. You'll never know if nobody asks.

### The "Inconsistent Execution" Problem
Your best rep follows up 5 times. Your worst rep follows up once. Your average rep follows up 2-3 times. Quality depends entirely on who the lead is assigned to.

Our system follows up the same way for every lead, every time, based on rules you set. Consistent execution. No variance. No luck of the draw.
