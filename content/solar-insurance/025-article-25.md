# ARTICLE 25
## Database Reactivation for Insurance: Turning Expired Policies Into Renewals

### Your Biggest Opportunity Is Hiding in Your Filing Cabinet

Every insurance agency has them: the expired policies. The lapsed coverage. The clients who didn't renew. The prospects who quoted but never bound. The people who moved, switched carriers, or just fell off the radar.

They're in your management system, your AMS, your CRM. Thousands of records. People who once trusted you with their insurance.

And you're spending money on Facebook ads to find new people while your former clients go uncontacted.

### Why Expired Policies Are Golden

Think about what an expired policy represents:

**They already know you.** They were your client. There's an existing relationship, even if it's dormant.

**They need insurance.** Insurance isn't optional. If they didn't renew with you, they went somewhere else — and that somewhere else might not be treating them well.

**Their situation changed.** Rates went up everywhere. Their new carrier's service might be terrible. They might have moved back to your area. Their needs might have changed.

**You have their data.** Name, address, phone, DOB, previous coverage, vehicles, property info. You know more about them than any cold lead could ever tell you.

**The cost to reach them is near-zero.** They're already in your system. No lead cost. Just the cost of a phone call or text.

### The AI Reactivation Playbook for Insurance

**Phase 1: Segment your database**

Not all expired policies are equal:
- **Lapsed < 6 months:** Highest priority. They're probably with another carrier but might be unhappy.
- **Lapsed 6-12 months:** Good potential. Enough time to be disappointed with their current carrier.
- **Lapsed 12-24 months:** Lower conversion rate but still worth contacting.
- **Lapsed 24+ months:** Contact info may be outdated. Still worth a try at negligible cost.

Also segment by type:
- **Auto-only:** Easy reactivation target. Rates change frequently.
- **Home + Auto bundle:** Higher value, worth more effort.
- **Life:** Different triggers (life events, age milestones).
- **Commercial:** Business is still operating? They need coverage.

**Phase 2: Personalized outreach**

AI reaches out with context-specific messaging:

For auto lapsed < 6 months:
"Hi [Name], it's been a while since we connected at [Agency]. With rates changing so much, I wanted to see if you're happy with your current auto coverage. Mind if I run a quick comparison? No obligation."

For home + auto lapsed 6-12 months:
"Hey [Name], [Agency] here. We noticed it's been about [X] months since your home and auto policies were with us. A lot has changed in the market — some carriers are offering much better rates now. Want me to run updated numbers?"

For life policy that lapsed:
"[Name], just reaching out from [Agency]. I know life gets busy, but I wanted to check in about your life insurance coverage. With [age milestone/life event season], it's a good time to make sure you're protected. Quick chat?"

**Phase 3: Multi-touch sequence**

Each former client gets 8-12 touches over 30 days:
- Day 1: Text + email
- Day 3: Call
- Day 5: Text with rate comparison angle
- Day 7: Call
- Day 10: Text with "shopping season" angle
- Day 14: Email with value content
- Day 21: Text with urgency
- Day 30: Final outreach

**Phase 4: Ongoing recycling**

Leads that don't convert re-enter the queue. Every 90 days, another outreach cycle. Circumstances change constantly in insurance — rate increases, life events, claims, dissatisfaction with current carrier.

### Real Agency Results

An independent P&C agency with 12,000 expired policies in their AMS ran an AI reactivation campaign:

- **Cost:** ~$800 (AI calling + texting)
- **Response rate:** 7.2% (864 conversations)
- **Requote rate:** 42% (363 requested new quotes)
- **Bind rate from requotes:** 31% (112 policies bound)
- **Average premium:** $2,200/year
- **New annual premium generated:** $246,400
- **Customer lifetime value (6 years):** $1,478,400

$800 in → $1.4M in lifetime premium out. That's not a typo.

And these are BETTER clients than new ones because:
- They already have a history with your agency
- They're less likely to leave again (they already tried another carrier and came back)
- They're easier to cross-sell (you have their data)
- Acquisition cost is basically zero

### The Quarterly Reactivation Machine

Smart agencies run reactivation quarterly:

- **Q1:** Target auto renewals coming up in Q2
- **Q2:** Target home renewals (often coincide with mortgage anniversaries)
- **Q3:** Target commercial renewals before year-end
- **Q4:** Target life insurance before year-end tax planning

Each quarter, AI sweeps the database with seasonally relevant messaging. The pipeline never dries up because you're constantly re-engaging former clients.

### Stop Chasing Strangers. Start Calling Friends.

Your former clients are easier to close, cheaper to acquire, and more likely to stay. Yet most agencies spend 90% of their marketing budget chasing strangers and 0% re-engaging former clients.

AI makes it effortless. Point it at your expired policies. Let it call, text, and email. Watch old relationships come back to life.

**Your best new clients are your old ones. Go get them back.**
