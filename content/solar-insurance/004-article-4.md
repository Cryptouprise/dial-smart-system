## The Solar Follow-Up Problem Nobody Talks About

### Every Solar Owner Knows This Feeling

You just dropped $40,000 on leads this month. Your team ripped through them. Some turned into appointments. Some didn't. And now those leads are sitting in your CRM with the status "attempted" or "no answer" or everyone's favorite — "not interested."

But here's what "not interested" really means 90% of the time: "Not interested right now, at this exact moment, when I'm making dinner and a random number called me."

That's not a dead lead. That's a lead you gave up on.

And I get it. I've run sales floors. I know why follow-up doesn't happen:

**Reps hate it.** Following up is the most soul-crushing part of sales. You're calling someone who didn't want to talk to you the first time, hoping they changed their mind. Most reps would rather eat glass.

**Fresh leads feel better.** There's a dopamine hit in ripping through a new list. It feels productive. It feels like grinding. Following up with Tuesday's leftovers? That feels like failure.

**There's no system.** Even if a rep wanted to follow up perfectly, who keeps track? You'd need to set 50 reminders a day, remember the context of each conversation, and maintain different cadences for different leads. Nobody does this manually.

**Math doesn't incentivize it.** Most commission structures reward appointments booked, not follow-ups completed. So reps go where the money is — fresh dials.

### The Real Cost of Broken Follow-Up

Here are numbers that should make you physically uncomfortable:

- **80% of sales happen after the 5th follow-up.** Your reps are making 1.5 attempts.
- **44% of salespeople give up after one follow-up.** ONE.
- **The average cost to acquire a new solar lead is $60-120.** And you're throwing it away after one call.
- **A followed-up lead is 7x more likely to convert** than a cold lead.

So your $80 lead that got one call attempt and was declared dead? It still has 7X the conversion potential of the next $80 lead you're about to buy.

But buying new leads FEELS proactive. Working old leads FEELS like scraping the bottom of the barrel. That feeling is costing you hundreds of thousands of dollars a year.

### The Anatomy of Perfect Solar Follow-Up

If I were running a solar sales floor today (and I basically am, through AI), here's what every single lead would get:

**Immediate Response (0-5 seconds):**
- Phone call
- Text message: "Hey [Name], this is [Company] — we're excited to help you explore solar for [Address]. Calling you now!"
- Email with company info, reviews, estimated savings

**Day 1 Follow-Up:**
- 2nd call attempt (4 hours after first)
- Text if no answer: "Missed you earlier — when's a good time to chat about cutting your electric bill?"

**Day 2-3:**
- Morning text with a value hook: "Homeowners in [City] are saving an avg of $180/mo with solar. Want to see what your roof could save?"
- Afternoon call

**Day 5-7:**
- Call with new angle
- Text: "Quick question — are you still looking into solar, or did you go another direction?"
- This text is gold because it creates urgency and gets responses from people who went quiet

**Day 10-14:**
- Email with case study or testimonial from their area
- Text: "Just checking in — solar incentives in [State] are changing soon. Happy to run your numbers real quick."

**Day 21-30:**
- Final push: "Last check-in — if solar's not for you right now, no worries at all. Just wanted to make sure you had all the info."

That's 12-15 touches across 30 days. Different channels. Different angles. Different times of day. Personalized to the lead's location and situation.

**Nobody is doing this manually.** Not your top rep. Not your sales manager. Nobody.
