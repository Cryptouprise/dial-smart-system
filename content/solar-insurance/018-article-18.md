## Aged Solar Leads for $0.03 Each. AI That Makes Them Worth $200.

### The Aged Lead Goldmine

Everyone in solar knows about aged leads. They're the leads that didn't convert for other companies — maybe 30 days old, maybe 90, maybe a year. You can buy them for pennies on the dollar.

Fresh lead: $60-120.
30-day aged lead: $5-15.
90-day aged lead: $1-5.
Year-old lead: $0.03-0.50.

Most solar companies look at those prices and think: "You get what you pay for." They assume aged leads are garbage — wrong numbers, tire kickers, people who already went solar with someone else.

Some of them are. But not all of them. Not even close.

### Why Aged Leads Are Undervalued

Here's what most people miss about aged leads:

**They've already raised their hand.** At some point, this person was interested enough in solar to fill out a form, click an ad, or take a call. That interest doesn't evaporate — it goes dormant.

**They were poorly followed up.** The previous company that bought this lead probably called once or twice and gave up. Sound familiar? If you have the same follow-up problem, yeah, they'll be garbage for you too. But with AI...

**Circumstances change.** The person who couldn't afford solar 6 months ago may have gotten a raise, refinanced their home, or gotten tired of $300 electric bills. The person whose spouse said no may have won them over after watching their neighbor save money.

**The competition gave up.** Nobody else is calling these people anymore. You have the field completely to yourself. No speed-to-lead race. No competing offers. Just you and a homeowner who once expressed interest.

### The AI Approach to Aged Leads

The reason aged leads have a bad reputation is that people work them the same way they work fresh leads — manual calls with a dialer. That doesn't work because:

1. The connect rate is lower (numbers change, people screen calls)
2. The context is different (they don't remember the inquiry)
3. The volume is too high for human follow-up
4. The conversion rate per individual lead is lower (but the cost is SO low it doesn't matter)

AI solves all of these:

**Volume:** AI can work 100,000 aged leads in a week. No human team can touch that.

**Multi-channel:** AI doesn't just call. It texts: "Hey [Name], we reached out a while back about solar for [Address]. Electricity rates in [City] went up 12% since then — want to see updated savings?" Text response rates on aged leads are actually higher than call connect rates.

**Persistence:** AI follows up 8-12 times over 30 days. The 3% that respond to the first text become 8-10% after the full sequence.

**Cost-per-attempt:** $0.02-0.05 per contact attempt. At those economics, even a 1% conversion rate is wildly profitable.

### The Aged Lead Playbook

**Step 1: Buy smart**
Focus on leads from your specific market area. Verify that the data includes name, phone, address, and ideally previous interest indicator.

**Step 2: Clean the list**
Run the numbers through a phone validation service. Remove disconnected numbers, landlines (for text campaigns), and duplicate entries. This might cost $0.01-0.02 per record.

**Step 3: AI text campaign first**
Start with text outreach — it's cheaper than calls and has higher response rates on aged data:
"Hi [Name], this is [Company] in [City]. We were looking at homes in your area for solar and yours looks like a great fit. Electric rates went up again — want to see what solar would save you?"

**Step 4: AI call campaign for responders + non-responders**
People who respond positively get a call from AI to qualify and book.
People who don't respond to texts get called.

**Step 5: Full follow-up sequence**
Every lead gets the full 8-12 touch treatment. No lead gets abandoned after one attempt.

**Step 6: Recurring sweeps**
Run the aged list again every 90 days. People who didn't respond in January might respond in April.
