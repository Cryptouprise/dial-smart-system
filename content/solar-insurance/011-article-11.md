# ARTICLE 11
## Solar Lead Cost Is $80. Your Follow-Up Rate Is 12%. Do the Math.

### The Most Expensive Waste in Solar

Here's a fun exercise: grab your last 30 days of lead spend and your CRM follow-up report. Put them side by side.

Lead spend: Let's say $60,000 (750 leads at $80 each).
Leads that received proper follow-up (5+ touch attempts): Maybe 90.

That's a 12% follow-up rate. Meaning 88% of your $60,000 — roughly **$52,800** — was spent on leads that got one or two halfhearted contact attempts before being abandoned.

You didn't buy bad leads. You bought good leads and treated them badly.

### Breaking Down the Waste

Let's follow the money through your sales funnel:

**$60,000** buys you 750 leads.

**First contact attempt:** Reps call all 750. 200 pick up. 550 go to voicemail or no answer.

Of the 200 who answered:
- 40 book appointments (5.3% of total leads — pretty standard)
- 30 say "not interested" (probably just busy or caught off guard)
- 50 say "call me back" or "send more info"
- 80 have decent conversations that don't lead anywhere yet

**Second contact attempt:** Maybe 200 of the 550 no-answers get a second call. Another 50 from the "call back" pile get attempted. The rest? Nobody's tracking them anymore.

**Third contact attempt:** What third attempt?

So out of 750 leads:
- 40 booked on first touch (5.3%)
- Maybe 10-15 more book from second attempts (1.5-2%)
- Total: ~50-55 appointments
- Total cost per appointment: ~$1,100

That's $1,100 per appointment when the lead cost was only $80. The other $1,020 is waste — wasted rep time, wasted follow-up opportunities, wasted second and third chances.

### The Follow-Up Economics

Data is crystal clear on this:

- **2% of sales happen on the 1st contact**
- **3% on the 2nd**
- **5% on the 3rd**
- **10% on the 4th**
- **80% on the 5th-12th contact**

You're giving up after 1.5 contacts on average. Which means you're capturing about 3-5% of the available opportunity and throwing away the other 95-97%.

If you followed up properly — 8-12 touches per lead — that same $60,000 in leads could generate:
- 150-200 appointments (instead of 50)
- Cost per appointment: $300-400 (instead of $1,100)
- 3-4X more revenue from the same lead spend

### Why Humans Won't Fix This

I'm not trying to beat up your reps. They're doing their best with an impossible job.

A setter working 8 hours can make maybe 150-200 dials per day. If they're supposed to follow up with every lead 8-12 times, and they get 750 new leads per month...

750 leads x 10 follow-ups = 7,500 follow-up attempts needed per month
200 dials/day x 22 working days = 4,400 available dials per month

The math literally doesn't work. Your reps physically cannot follow up properly while also working new leads. So they triage — new leads first, follow-ups if they have time. They never have time.

This isn't a motivation problem. It's a math problem. And math problems require math solutions.

### The AI Fix

AI doesn't have a dial limit. It doesn't have to choose between new leads and follow-ups. It does both simultaneously.

Every single lead that enters your system gets:
- 5-second speed to lead response
- A complete 8-12 touch sequence over 30 days
- Multi-channel outreach (call, text, email)
- Personalized messaging based on previous interactions
- Automatic disposition and pipeline management

The result? Every dollar of that $60,000 in lead spend actually gets worked properly.

Instead of your $80 lead getting one call and being declared dead, it gets the full treatment — 12 touches, multiple channels, personalized follow-up, intelligent re-engagement.

Your follow-up rate goes from 12% to 100%. Not because your reps work harder. Because a machine does the work that was never humanly possible in the first place.

### The Real Cost of "Good Enough"

Every solar owner I talk to says some version of: "We could be better at follow-up, but we're doing okay."

"Okay" is leaving 70-80% of your revenue on the table. "Okay" is spending $60K on leads and getting $15K worth of value. "Okay" is your competitors hiring AI and eating your lunch while you think your 12% follow-up rate is acceptable.

It's not okay. It's expensive. And it's fixable.

**$80 per lead. 12% follow-up rate. The math doesn't lie. Fix the follow-up or keep burning money. Your choice.**
