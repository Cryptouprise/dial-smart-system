# 19. "It's Too Expensive" — The Math on a Missed Lead

Let's do math. I know attorneys love arguing, so argue with the numbers.

**The Cost of a Lead**

What do you spend on marketing? Let's say $10,000 per month on Google Ads for personal injury leads. You get 100 leads. That's $100 per lead.

How many convert to clients? Industry average for law firms is 20-30%. Let's be generous and say 25%. That's 25 clients per month.

What happened to the other 75 leads? They called and got voicemail. They filled out a form at 9 PM. They didn't get a callback fast enough. They got a generic auto-response and called someone else. They needed follow-up and nobody followed up.

You paid $7,500 for those 75 leads and got nothing.

**The Cost of AI**

Our system costs a fraction of a single employee's salary. For the sake of argument, let's say it's a few thousand dollars per month. Now let's say it improves your conversion rate from 25% to 35%. That's not aggressive — that's what happens when you respond in 5 seconds and follow up 15 times instead of twice.

35% of 100 leads = 35 clients. That's 10 additional clients per month.

**The Revenue of Those 10 Clients**

Personal injury: 10 cases x $50,000 average fee = $500,000
Family law: 10 cases x $5,000 average retainer = $50,000
Criminal defense: 10 cases x $5,000 average retainer = $50,000
Bankruptcy: 10 cases x $3,000 average fee = $30,000

Whatever your practice area, 10 extra clients per month is real money. And the AI costs less than what you spend on coffee for the office.

**The Real Expense: Doing Nothing**

The most expensive thing in your firm isn't the AI. It's the leads you already paid for that are rotting in your CRM. Every missed call, every delayed response, every dropped follow-up — that's money you already spent, walking out the door.

You're not saving money by not having AI. You're hemorrhaging it. Slowly. Invisibly. Every single day.

**The Employee Comparison**

Want to hire a human to do what AI does? Great.

- Salary: $45,000-$60,000/year
- Benefits: $10,000-$15,000/year
- Training: $5,000
- Turnover (and you WILL have turnover): $10,000-$20,000 per replacement
- Works 8 hours a day, 5 days a week
- Calls in sick
- Takes vacation
- Has bad days

AI costs less, works 24/7, never calls in sick, and handles 10x the volume.

"Too expensive" isn't a math problem. It's a perception problem. And perception doesn't pay the bills.
