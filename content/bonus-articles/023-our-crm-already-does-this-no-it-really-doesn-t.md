# 23. "Our CRM Already Does This" — No, It Really Doesn't

"We have Clio. It handles our intake."

No. Clio handles your case management. Your intake is a form on your website that nobody responds to until tomorrow.

Let me explain the difference between a CRM and an AI intake system, because this confusion is costing you cases.

**What Your CRM Does:**

- Stores contact information
- Manages case files and documents
- Tracks billable hours
- Sends invoices
- Maybe has a basic form on your website
- Maybe sends an auto-response email
- Organizes existing clients

**What Your CRM Does NOT Do:**

- Call leads in 5 seconds
- Have voice conversations with potential clients
- Qualify leads based on practice area criteria
- Answer questions about your firm
- Book consultations on your calendar
- Follow up via call, text, and email automatically
- Re-engage dead leads from your database
- Warm-transfer qualified leads to attorneys
- Handle after-hours intake
- Reduce no-shows with automated reminders

**The Form-to-Client Gap**

Here's the journey of a lead in your CRM: Form fill → auto-response email → sits in inbox → someone sees it tomorrow → calls back → no answer → calls back again → maybe gets through → starts intake from scratch.

Here's the journey with AI: Form fill → AI calls in 5 seconds → full intake conversation → qualified → consultation booked → confirmation sent → reminders scheduled → attorney walks into consultation with full context.

One of those journeys loses 60% of leads. The other loses 10%.

**The "Smart CRM" Myth**

Some CRMs now have "AI features" — automated emails, chatbots, workflow triggers. These are bolt-on features trying to mimic what a purpose-built AI intake system does natively.

It's the difference between a Swiss Army knife and a chef's knife. The Swiss Army knife technically has a blade. But if you're trying to run a kitchen, you need the real thing.

**The Integration Reality**

Our AI doesn't replace your CRM. It supercharges it. Leads flow from AI into Clio, MyCase, PracticePanther, Salesforce, GHL, or whatever you use. Your CRM still manages cases. AI manages the part your CRM was never designed to handle — the conversion from stranger to client.

Your CRM is a filing cabinet. AI is the salesperson who fills it.
