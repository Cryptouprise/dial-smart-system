# 29. Lead Decay Curves: What Happens to Your Leads After 24 Hours

I want to show you what happens to a lead's likelihood of conversion as time passes. It's not a slow decline. It's a cliff.

**The Decay Timeline:**

- **0-5 minutes:** Lead is hot. They're actively looking. Conversion potential is at maximum.
- **5-30 minutes:** Lead is cooling. They may have contacted other firms. Conversion drops 50%.
- **30-60 minutes:** Lead is lukewarm. The emotional impulse is fading. Conversion drops 75%.
- **1-24 hours:** Lead is cold. They've either hired someone else or the urgency has passed. Conversion drops 90%.
- **24-72 hours:** Lead is nearly dead. You'd need significant follow-up to revive. Conversion drops 95%.
- **72+ hours:** Lead is ice cold. They've forgotten they called you. The matter may have resolved itself, been handled by another firm, or been abandoned entirely.

**The Math of Decay**

If 100% of your leads have "conversion potential" at minute zero, and 80% of that potential evaporates within 5 minutes of no response, your effective lead pool is 20% of what you paid for within minutes of acquisition.

You spent $100 per lead. By the time you call back tomorrow, that lead is worth $5.

**Why Legal Leads Decay Faster**

Legal leads decay faster than most industries because:

1. **High emotional stakes.** The person calling was in an emotional state. Emotion drives action. When the emotion fades, so does the action.
2. **Multiple firm contact.** 78% of legal leads contact more than one firm. The first meaningful response wins.
3. **Procrastination patterns.** Many legal issues (estate planning, business formation) can be put off indefinitely. If you don't capture the person when they're motivated, they'll procrastinate for another 6 months.
4. **Resolution by other means.** Some problems resolve themselves — insurance pays, charges get dropped, spouse cools down. The longer you wait, the higher the chance the lead self-resolves.

**Reversing the Curve**

The only way to reverse lead decay is to make contact within the first 5 minutes and maintain consistent follow-up for leads that don't convert immediately.

AI does both. Contact in 5 seconds. Follow-up for 30, 60, 90 days. The decay curve gets inverted — instead of losing leads over time, you're recovering them.

**What This Means for Your Marketing Budget**

If you're spending $10,000/month on marketing and your average response time is 4 hours, roughly 90% of your lead value is evaporating before you make contact. That's $9,000/month in effectively wasted spend.

Speed to lead isn't a nice-to-have. It's the difference between a profitable firm and one that's slowly bleeding cash through delayed responses.
