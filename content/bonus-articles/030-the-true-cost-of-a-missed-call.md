# 30. The True Cost of a Missed Call

What's a missed call worth? Not what it costs you to miss it — what it costs you in lost revenue.

Let me break it down practice area by practice area, because I want this to hurt.

**Personal Injury:**
- Average case value: $50,000-$100,000 in fees
- Average missed calls per firm per month: 15-20
- If even 20% of those would have converted: 3-4 missed cases
- Cost: $150,000-$400,000/year in lost fees

**Family Law:**
- Average retainer: $3,000-$10,000
- Average missed calls per month: 15-20
- If 25% would have converted: 4-5 missed cases
- Cost: $12,000-$50,000/year in lost retainers

**Criminal Defense:**
- Average retainer: $2,500-$15,000
- Missed after-hours calls per month (the money calls): 20-30
- If 30% would have converted: 6-9 missed cases
- Cost: $15,000-$135,000/year in lost retainers

**The Compound Effect**

These numbers don't account for referrals. Every client you DON'T get is also every referral they would have sent you. A satisfied criminal defense client might refer 3-5 people over the next few years. Those referrals are free leads with high conversion rates.

When you miss a call, you're not losing one client. You're losing a client and their entire referral tree.

**Where the Calls Go**

When your phone goes to voicemail, the caller does one of three things:

1. **Calls the next firm on Google** (60% of callers). They're gone in 30 seconds.
2. **Leaves a voicemail and waits** (25% of callers). By the time you call back, half have already hired someone else.
3. **Gives up entirely** (15% of callers). The legal need goes unaddressed. This is the saddest outcome.

**The AI Solution**

AI answers every call. First ring. 24/7/365. The concept of a "missed call" ceases to exist.

Every firm has a number they can tolerate in overhead. But nobody should tolerate losing revenue because the phone rang and nobody picked up. That's not overhead — that's negligence with a profit margin.
