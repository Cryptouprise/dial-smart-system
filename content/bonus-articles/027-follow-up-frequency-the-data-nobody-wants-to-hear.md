# 27. Follow-Up Frequency: The Data Nobody Wants to Hear

Here's a stat that kills me: 48% of salespeople (and law firm intake people are salespeople, whether they admit it or not) never follow up with a prospect. Not once.

Of the ones who do follow up:

- 25% make a second contact and stop
- 12% make a third contact and stop
- Only 10% make more than three contacts

Now here's the kicker: 80% of sales (and client conversions) happen between the 5th and 12th contact.

Read that again. Most firms stop at 1-2 follow-ups. Most conversions happen at 5-12 follow-ups. THE MATH DOES NOT MATH.

**Why Law Firms Are Terrible at Follow-Up**

It's not because they don't want to follow up. It's because:

1. **New leads feel more exciting than old ones.** Fresh form fill at the top of the inbox? Call that one. The lead from 2 weeks ago who didn't answer? Eh, they're probably not interested.

2. **It feels awkward.** Nobody wants to be "that guy" calling for the 8th time. Attorneys especially don't want to seem desperate.

3. **There's no system.** Follow-up lives in someone's head, on sticky notes, or in a CRM that nobody checks regularly.

4. **Staff turnover kills it.** Your intake person quits. The new person doesn't know about the 47 leads that needed follow-up this week.

**The Fortune in the Follow-Up**

Every sales trainer on earth has said it: the fortune is in the follow-up. But no one acts on it because follow-up is boring, repetitive, and thankless. Until it works. Then it's the most profitable activity in your firm.

**AI and the Follow-Up Advantage**

AI doesn't get bored. AI doesn't feel awkward. AI doesn't forget. AI follows up exactly as many times as you tell it to, on exactly the schedule you set, through exactly the channels you choose.

Text on day 1. Call on day 3. Email on day 5. Text on day 7. Call on day 10. Email on day 14. Text on day 21. Call on day 30.

Each touchpoint is contextual. Not a copy-paste "just following up." AI remembers the conversation and references it: "Last time we spoke, you mentioned you were waiting for the medical records. Were you able to get those?"

**The Compounding Effect**

Follow-up isn't linear. It compounds. The lead who doesn't respond to text #1 might respond to call #5 because they finally have a free moment. The lead who ghosted in January might need help in March. AI is still there.

Your competitors follow up once and move on. You follow up 15 times. Who wins more clients?
