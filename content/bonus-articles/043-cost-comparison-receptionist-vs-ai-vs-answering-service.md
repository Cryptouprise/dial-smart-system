# 43. Cost Comparison: Receptionist vs. AI vs. Answering Service vs. Nothing

Let's put the options side by side. No spin. Just numbers.

**Option 1: Full-Time Receptionist**

- Salary: $35,000-$50,000/year
- Benefits: $8,000-$15,000/year
- Training: $2,000-$5,000
- Total annual cost: $45,000-$70,000
- Hours covered: 8 hours/day, 5 days/week (2,080 hours/year)
- Cost per hour: $22-$34
- Calls handled per day: 30-50
- After-hours coverage: None
- Follow-up capability: Limited (1-2 attempts)
- Qualification accuracy: Variable (depends on training and mood)
- Sick days: 5-10/year
- Vacation: 10-15 days/year
- Turnover: Every 12-18 months

**Option 2: Answering Service**

- Monthly cost: $200-$800/month ($2,400-$9,600/year)
- Hours covered: 24/7
- What they do: Answer phone, take message, forward to you
- Qualification: None
- Follow-up: None
- Appointment booking: None (usually)
- CRM integration: None (usually)
- Quality: Inconsistent (different operator each time)
- Knowledge of your firm: Minimal

**Option 3: AI Intake System**

- Monthly cost: Fraction of a receptionist salary
- Hours covered: 24/7/365
- What it does: Answers, qualifies, books, follows up, integrates, reports
- Qualification: Full practice-area-specific qualifying
- Follow-up: 10-20+ automated touches across call, text, email
- Appointment booking: Real-time calendar integration
- CRM integration: Full (GHL, Clio, Salesforce, etc.)
- Quality: Consistent (same quality at 3 AM as 3 PM)
- Knowledge of your firm: Complete (trained on your specific practice)
- Sick days: Zero
- Vacation: Zero
- Turnover: Zero

**Option 4: Nothing (Voicemail)**

- Monthly cost: $0
- What it does: Records a message that gets listened to eventually
- Leads lost: 60-80% of after-hours inquiries
- Revenue lost: Tens of thousands per month
- Professional impression: "They must not be very busy/good"

**The Verdict**

A receptionist is necessary for in-office operations but insufficient for lead capture. An answering service is a band-aid. AI is the solution. And "nothing" is financial negligence.

The smart move: Keep your receptionist for in-office work. Replace or supplement the answering service with AI. Capture every lead, convert more clients, and stop lighting money on fire.
