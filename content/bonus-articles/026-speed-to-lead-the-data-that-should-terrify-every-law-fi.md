# 26. Speed to Lead: The Data That Should Terrify Every Law Firm

Let me hit you with a number that should make you physically uncomfortable: after 5 minutes, your chances of qualifying a lead drop by 80%.

Five minutes. Not five hours. Not five days. Five minutes.

**The Harvard Business Review Study**

The most cited study on lead response time comes from research by Dr. James Oldroyd at MIT. He analyzed over 100,000 call attempts across multiple industries and found:

- Responding within 5 minutes: 21x more likely to qualify the lead than waiting 30 minutes
- Responding within 1 minute: 391% more likely to convert than waiting even 4 minutes
- After 5 minutes: qualification rates plummet and never recover

These aren't legal-specific numbers. They're WORSE for legal, because legal leads are often in emotional states that cool quickly.

**The Law Firm Reality**

What's the average response time for law firms? Studies show it's 24-48 hours. Some firms take 3-5 days to respond to a web form submission.

Let that sink in. The data says 5 minutes is the window. The average firm responds in 24 hours. That's not a gap — that's a canyon.

**Why Speed Matters More in Legal**

Legal leads are uniquely time-sensitive because:

1. **Emotional state drives action.** People call lawyers during emotional peaks — after an accident, after an arrest, during a family crisis. That emotional peak fades fast.

2. **They're shopping.** 78% of legal consumers contact multiple firms. The first firm to respond meaningfully gets an outsized advantage.

3. **Decision fatigue sets in.** After talking to 2-3 firms, people stop calling. They either hire someone or give up.

4. **Competitor response times are improving.** The firms that adopt AI respond in seconds. If your competitor responds in 5 seconds and you respond in 5 hours, it's not even a competition.

**What 5-Second Response Looks Like**

Our system contacts leads within 5 seconds of form submission, call, or chat initiation. Call, text, AND email — simultaneously. The lead isn't waiting. They're in a conversation before they've even closed the browser tab.

At $5,000 per client (conservative for most practice areas), that's $75,000 per month in additional revenue.

Because you answered the phone faster.
