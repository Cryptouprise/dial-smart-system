# 42. Before and After: What AI Intake Actually Changes

Let me show you what a real law firm looks like before AI intake and six months after. Names changed, results real.

**BEFORE: The Johnson Law Group**

Practices: Family law and criminal defense
Marketing spend: $8,000/month (Google Ads + LSA)
Monthly leads: 60
Team: 2 attorneys, 1 paralegal, 1 receptionist
Response time: 2-6 hours (business hours only)
Conversion rate: 18%
Monthly new clients: 11
Average case value: $4,500
Monthly revenue from new clients: $49,500

Pain points:
- Receptionist overwhelmed by phone volume
- After-hours leads (40+/month) went to voicemail
- Follow-up was inconsistent — paralegal did it "when she had time"
- No-show rate: 35%
- Database of 2,000+ old leads never contacted

**AFTER: 6 Months with AI Intake**

Same marketing spend: $8,000/month
Same lead volume: 60/month
Response time: 5 seconds (24/7)
Conversion rate: 31%
Monthly new clients: 19
Average case value: $4,500 (unchanged)
Monthly revenue from new clients: $85,500

Additional improvements:
- After-hours leads converted at the same rate as business hours
- No-show rate dropped to 12%
- Database reactivation campaign generated 14 additional clients in the first 3 months
- Receptionist reassigned to client services (happier, more productive)
- Paralegal focused on case work instead of follow-up calls

**Revenue Impact:**

- Before AI: $49,500/month in new client revenue
- After AI: $85,500/month in new client revenue
- Increase: $36,000/month = $432,000/year
- Plus database reactivation: ~$63,000 in the first 3 months

**What Changed:**

The firm didn't get better at marketing. They didn't hire more staff. They didn't change their practice areas. They just stopped losing the leads they were already generating.

That's it. They plugged the holes. AI plugged the holes.

The before/after isn't dramatic because they did something revolutionary. It's dramatic because they stopped doing something stupid — which was letting leads die in voicemail.
