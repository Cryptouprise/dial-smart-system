# 34. Multilingual Intake: Stop Losing Clients Who Don't Speak English

If you practice in a major metropolitan area, a significant percentage of your potential clients speak Spanish, Mandarin, Vietnamese, Korean, Tagalog, Arabic, or one of dozens of other languages.

And your intake process speaks exactly one language: English.

How many leads do you think call your firm, realize they can't communicate effectively, and hang up? The answer is: more than you want to know.

**The Market You're Missing**

Let's look at numbers:

- 67 million people in the US speak a language other than English at home
- 25 million are classified as "limited English proficient"
- Hispanic Americans are the fastest-growing demographic in the US
- In major legal markets (LA, Miami, Houston, NYC, Chicago), 30-50% of potential clients are bilingual or non-English speaking

If your intake can't handle Spanish, you're losing 20-30% of your addressable market in most major cities. Not because these people don't need attorneys — because they can't navigate your English-only intake process.

**The Interpreter Workaround Doesn't Work**

"We use Language Line when we get a Spanish speaker."

How's that working? You put the client on hold. You dial the interpreter service. You wait 3-5 minutes for an interpreter. You have a three-way conversation where everything takes twice as long and half the nuance is lost.

The client feels like a burden. The call takes 30 minutes instead of 10. Your receptionist is tied up and can't answer other calls. And the interpreter doesn't know your practice areas, your intake criteria, or your firm.

**How AI Handles Multilingual Intake**

AI switches languages naturally. If a Spanish speaker calls, AI conducts the entire intake in Spanish. Not clunky translated scripts — actual conversational Spanish (or Mandarin, or Portuguese, or whatever languages you configure).

The lead has a comfortable, natural conversation in their preferred language. All the data is captured in English for your attorneys. The consultation gets booked. The confirmation goes out in the client's preferred language.

Zero friction. Zero delay. Zero interpreters on hold.

**The Loyalty Factor**

When a non-English speaker finds a firm that communicates in their language seamlessly, they don't shop around. They're loyal. They refer everyone they know. In immigrant communities especially, word-of-mouth is the most powerful marketing channel — and it's powered by positive experience.

One Spanish-speaking client who has a great intake experience can generate 10+ referrals within their community.

**The Practice Area Impact**

Multilingual intake is especially critical for:
- Immigration law (obvious)
- Workers' compensation (construction, agriculture, food service workers)
- Personal injury (high representation in manual labor industries)
- Criminal defense (language barriers during arrest add complexity)
- Family law (international custody, immigration-related divorces)

AI speaks the language your clients speak. That's not a feature — it's a fundamental requirement for any firm serious about serving their market.
