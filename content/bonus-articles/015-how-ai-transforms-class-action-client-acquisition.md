# 15. How AI Transforms Class Action Client Acquisition

Class action is the ultimate volume play, and most firms are drowning in it.

When you file a class action or start advertising for plaintiffs, you don't get 10 leads. You get 10,000. Sometimes 100,000. You need to qualify every single one. Quickly. Accurately. Consistently.

Your intake team of 6 people isn't going to cut it.

**The Flood**

Class action advertising — whether it's a data breach, product liability, consumer fraud, or employment class action — generates massive spikes of leads. TV ads, social media, web campaigns — when it hits, your phone doesn't stop ringing.

Each of those calls needs to be:
1. Answered (not voicemail'd)
2. Qualified against specific criteria
3. Documented with consistent data
4. Signed if qualified
5. Followed up if incomplete

That's not a human-scale problem. That's an AI-scale problem.

**The Consistency Issue**

When humans handle high-volume intake, quality degrades. Call #1 of the day gets thorough questioning. Call #347 gets rushed. Important qualification criteria gets skipped. Data gets entered inconsistently. Cases that should have been signed slip through.

AI doesn't get tired. Call #1 and call #10,000 get identical treatment. Every qualifying question asked. Every data field completed. Every potential plaintiff treated with the same care and attention.

**The Qualification Matrix**

Class actions have strict qualification criteria:

- Were they affected by the specific incident/product/behavior?
- During the relevant time period?
- In the relevant geography?
- Can they provide proof of purchase/usage/exposure?
- Do they have documented damages?

AI applies this matrix consistently to every lead, no exceptions. Qualified leads get signed. Unqualified leads get a clear, professional explanation of why they don't meet the criteria.

**The Data Integrity**

In class action litigation, data integrity is everything. When you're presenting a class of 50,000 plaintiffs to a judge, you need clean, consistent data. AI collects information in a standardized format every time. No typos, no missed fields, no "I forgot to ask about the purchase date."

**The Follow-Up at Scale**

Many class action leads need to provide documentation — proof of purchase, medical records, employment records. AI follows up with each plaintiff individually to collect missing documents. "Hi Lisa, we still need a copy of your purchase receipt for the [product] class action. You can take a photo and text it to this number."

Multiply that by 10,000 plaintiffs. No human team on earth.

**The Business Case**

Class actions are funded by volume. More qualified plaintiffs = bigger class = bigger settlement = bigger fees. The math is simple. If AI helps you qualify and sign 20% more plaintiffs, your settlement value increases proportionally.

Class action is where AI stops being a "nice to have" and becomes the only way to run a viable operation.
