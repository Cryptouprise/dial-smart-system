# 48. What 10 Million+ Calls Taught Us About Legal Intake

We've made over 10 million calls across every industry. Here's what we've learned that applies specifically to law firms.

**Lesson 1: The First 5 Seconds Are Everything**

Not the first 5 minutes. The first 5 seconds. The moment someone feels heard — even before they explain their problem — they relax. The opening line matters more than anything else in the conversation.

What works: "Hi [Name], thank you for reaching out to [Firm Name]. I'd like to help you with your situation."

What doesn't work: "This is a recorded line. Please state your name, the nature of your inquiry, and the best time to reach you."

People want to feel like they called a person, not a system. Even when it IS a system.

**Lesson 2: Ask Permission Before Questions**

The most effective intake conversations start with "Do you have a few minutes to answer some questions so we can connect you with the right attorney?" This simple permission request increases completion rates by 35%.

Why? Because people feel in control. They said yes. They committed. Now they'll answer your 10 qualifying questions because they already agreed to the process.

**Lesson 3: Empathy Before Efficiency**

When someone says "I was in a car accident," the worst response is "When did the accident occur?" The best response is "I'm sorry to hear that. Are you okay?" followed by "When did the accident occur?"

Two seconds of empathy. 40% higher engagement.

**Lesson 4: Text > Call for Follow-Up**

For initial contact, calls win. For follow-up, text wins. Specifically:

- Initial contact: Call + text simultaneously (call for engagement, text for backup)
- Follow-up #1 (day 1): Text
- Follow-up #2 (day 3): Call
- Follow-up #3 (day 7): Text
- Subsequent: Alternate text and call

People are more likely to respond to texts because they can do it on their own time. Calls feel intrusive after the initial contact.

**Lesson 5: Evenings Convert Better Than Mornings**

Counter-intuitive, but 7-9 PM is the highest-converting time window for follow-up contacts. People are home, relaxed, and have time to talk. 9-10 AM is the worst — people are rushing into their day.

**Lesson 6: The 7th Follow-Up Is the Magic Number**

Across 10 million+ calls, the highest conversion spike after initial contact occurs on the 7th follow-up attempt. Not the 2nd. Not the 5th. The 7th. Most firms give up after 2.

**Lesson 7: Confirmation Texts Reduce No-Shows by 50%**

A simple confirmation text after booking — with date, time, location, and attorney name — cuts no-show rates in half. Add a reminder text 2 hours before and you cut them by another 25%.

These aren't opinions. They're patterns extracted from millions of data points. And they're built into every AI intake system we deploy.
