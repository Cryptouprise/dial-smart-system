# 44. Time Savings: Where AI Gives Your Attorneys Hours Back

Let me ask you something: how many hours per week do your attorneys spend on intake-related activities?

Think about it:
- Returning calls from potential clients
- Doing intake interviews that could be handled by a trained system
- Playing phone tag with leads who don't answer
- Reviewing voicemail messages and deciding who to call first
- Re-doing intake because the initial person didn't get enough information
- Managing their own follow-up because the staff dropped the ball

For most attorneys, it's 5-10 hours per week. At a billing rate of $300-$500/hour, that's $1,500-$5,000 per week in lost billable time. That's $78,000-$260,000 per year.

PER ATTORNEY.

**The Time Audit**

Let's break down where the time goes:

- Returning voicemails: 30 min/day = 2.5 hours/week
- Initial intake conversations: 45 min/day = 3.75 hours/week
- Follow-up calls: 20 min/day = 1.67 hours/week
- Scheduling coordination: 15 min/day = 1.25 hours/week
- **Total: 9+ hours/week on intake-related tasks**

That's more than a full business day. Every week. Spent on work that AI handles better.

**What AI Replaces:**

- All voicemail return calls (AI already had the conversation)
- 80% of initial intake (AI gathers everything, attorney just reviews)
- All follow-up calls (AI handles automatically)
- All scheduling (AI books directly on the calendar)

**Hours recovered per attorney: 7-8 hours/week**

**Where Those Hours Go Instead:**

- More billable work ($2,100-$4,000/week in additional billing at $300-$500/hr)
- Better case preparation (higher win rates, better outcomes)
- Client relationship management (better reviews, more referrals)
- Business development (growing the firm instead of managing the phone)
- Or — here's a radical thought — leaving the office at 5 PM

**The Quality Improvement**

When attorneys aren't distracted by intake tasks, their legal work improves. They're more focused in depositions. They prepare better for hearings. They catch details they would have missed while multitasking.

AI doesn't just save time. It improves the quality of everything else the attorney does with that time.

**The Firm-Wide Impact**

For a 5-attorney firm: 7 hours/week x 5 attorneys x 50 weeks = 1,750 hours/year recovered. At $300/hour average billing rate, that's $525,000 in potential additional billing.

Half a million dollars. In time. That you're currently spending on phone tag.
