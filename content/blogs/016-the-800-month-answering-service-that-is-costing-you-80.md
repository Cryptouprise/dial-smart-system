## What Answering Services Actually Do (And Do Not Do)

Let us be clear about what you are paying for with a traditional answering service:

**What they do:**
- Answer the phone with your firm's name
- Take down a name and phone number
- Write a brief message
- Email or text you the message

**What they do not do:**
- Qualify the lead
- Ask practice-area-specific intake questions
- Determine if the case fits your firm's criteria
- Book appointments on your calendar
- Send follow-up texts or emails
- Re-engage if the prospect does not answer your callback
- Warm-transfer qualified leads to an available attorney
- Provide any information about your firm, your attorneys, or your process
- Follow up. At all. Ever.

In other words, they are a human voicemail machine. They turn a phone call into a message. That is the entire value proposition.

For $500-1,500 per month.

## What AI Intake Does (The Full List)

Now compare that to what an AI intake system handles:

### Immediate, Intelligent Conversation

The AI does not take a message. It has a conversation. It asks about the legal situation, listens to the responses, asks follow-up questions, and gathers everything an intake coordinator would need.

For a personal injury call: "Can you tell me about the accident? When did it happen? Were there any witnesses? Have you seen a doctor? Has the insurance company contacted you?"

For a family law call: "What is the nature of your family law matter? Are there children involved? Has anything been filed yet?"

### Real-Time Qualification

The AI evaluates the lead against your firm's criteria. Does this case fit your practice areas? Does the timeline work? Are there any red flags? If the case does not fit, the AI can provide a warm referral to a partner firm. If it does fit, the process continues seamlessly.

### Appointment Booking

The AI accesses your attorneys' actual calendars and books consultations in real time. The prospect gets a confirmed appointment before they hang up the phone — not a "someone will call you back" that may or may not happen.

### Warm Transfer

If an attorney is available, the AI transfers the call directly. But not a cold transfer where the attorney has to start from scratch. The AI briefs the attorney: "I have a potential client on the line — slip and fall at a grocery store two days ago, currently treating, no other attorney contacted, looking for representation."

### Follow-Up Automation

After the call, the AI sends:
- A confirmation text with appointment details
- An email with your firm's information and what to bring to the consultation
- Reminder sequences at 72 hours, 24 hours, 2 hours, and 30 minutes before the appointment
- If the prospect does not book, a nurture sequence that re-engages over the following days and weeks

### Outbound Capability

Answering services sit and wait for the phone to ring. AI intake is proactive. It can call leads who filled out a web form. It can follow up on leads from last week. It can re-engage old prospects from your database.

It does not just "know your firm." It operates as a full member of your intake team.
