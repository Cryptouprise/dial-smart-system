## The Empty Chair Problem

There is a particular kind of frustration that every managing partner knows. You spent $200 to generate the lead. Your intake team spent 20 minutes qualifying them. You blocked 45 minutes on an attorney's calendar. The conference room is set up. The coffee is poured.

And the chair across the table is empty.

The prospect did not show up. Did not call. Did not text. Just... did not come.

Your attorney sits there for 10 minutes, checks their phone, sends one email to the prospect — "We missed you today, would you like to reschedule?" — and moves on. That slot on the calendar is gone forever. That lead is almost certainly gone forever too.

**For most law firms, this happens on 25-40% of scheduled consultations.** Read that again. A quarter to nearly half of every consultation your firm books results in an empty chair and wasted attorney time.

- **40 consultations scheduled per month**

That is not a typo. **$112,000 per month in lost revenue** from people who were interested enough to schedule a consultation but did not show up. Over a year, that is $1.34 million. For a firm that books 40 consultations per month.

And that does not include the marketing spend wasted on generating those leads, the staff time spent on intake and scheduling, or the demoralizing effect on your team.

## Why People No-Show (It Is Not What You Think)

The natural assumption is that no-shows are tire-kickers who were never serious. That is sometimes true. But more often, the reasons are mundane and fixable:

### They Forgot

This is the number one reason. The consultation was booked on Monday for Thursday. By Thursday, life happened. The kids got sick. Work got crazy. They simply forgot they had an appointment at your office at 2 PM.

### They Got Nervous

Legal consultations are intimidating. People worry about cost, about being judged, about hearing bad news about their case. Between booking the appointment and the day it arrives, anxiety builds. Without any touchpoint from your firm to reassure them, they talk themselves out of it.

### They Found Another Firm

If three days passed between booking and the appointment with zero communication from your firm, that is three days where another firm's ad, another firm's follow-up, another firm's Google review could have stolen them.

### The Logistics Were Wrong

They booked a 2 PM appointment and then realized they cannot get off work. But they do not want to call to reschedule because that feels awkward. So they just... do not show up.

### They Resolved It Themselves

The insurance company made an offer. Their spouse talked them out of pursuing the case. The injury turned out to be minor. These are genuine no-shows, but they are a minority — and even some of these people would benefit from a consultation if someone reminded them why they scheduled it in the first place.

## The Fix: AI-Powered Appointment Management

Here is what a proper no-show prevention and recovery system looks like:

### Prevention: The Reminder Sequence

**72 hours before the appointment:**
Email with a subject line like "Your consultation with [Attorney Name] is coming up on Thursday." Include the date, time, location/call-in details, what to bring (ID, police report, medical records, insurance information), and a one-click reschedule option.

**24 hours before:**
Text message: "Hi [Name], just a reminder about your consultation with [Attorney Name] tomorrow at 2:00 PM at [location]. Reply YES to confirm or RESCHEDULE if you need a different time."

This is critical. The text gives them an easy out. If they cannot make it, they can reschedule right there. That is infinitely better than a no-show.

**2 hours before:**
Text message: "Looking forward to meeting you in 2 hours. Here's the address: [address]. Parking is available in the lot behind the building. See you soon!"

**30 minutes before:**
Final text: "Your consultation starts in 30 minutes. If you're running late, no worries — just let us know and we'll adjust."

### Recovery: When They Still No-Show

Despite the best reminders, some people will still miss their appointment. What happens in the next 15 minutes determines whether you lose that lead forever or save it.

**5 minutes after the appointment time:**
AI sends a text: "Hi [Name], we noticed you weren't able to make your consultation today. No worries at all — would you like to reschedule? I have availability tomorrow at [time] or [time]."

**15 minutes after:**
AI calls the prospect. "Hi [Name], this is [Firm Name] calling. We had you down for a consultation today and wanted to make sure everything is okay. Would you like to find a new time? We want to make sure we can help you with your [case type]."

**Same day, evening:**
AI sends an email: "We missed you today. Here are three available consultation times this week — just click one to rebook instantly."

**Day +1, +3, +7:**
Follow-up sequence continues. Each touchpoint is warm, professional, and makes it easy to reschedule. No guilt-tripping. No pressure. Just persistent availability.

## Why Humans Cannot Do This Consistently

Your intake coordinator could, in theory, send reminders and make recovery calls manually. But they will not. Not consistently.

Manual reminders mean:
- Checking the calendar each morning
- Sending individual texts to each person with an appointment tomorrow
- Remembering to send the 2-hour and 30-minute reminders
- Noticing no-shows in real-time and immediately calling them
- Following up on Day 1, Day 3, Day 7

This is exactly the kind of repetitive, time-sensitive, detail-oriented work that falls apart when your intake coordinator is on the phone with a new lead, at lunch, on vacation, or just having a busy day. One missed reminder, one delayed recovery call, and the lead is gone.

AI does this for every appointment, every time, without exception. It does not have busy days. It does not forget. It does not decide that a particular lead "probably won't show anyway."
