## The Most Expensive Phone Call in Your Firm

Let us do some quick math that will ruin your afternoon.

A senior attorney at a mid-size firm bills at $400-600 per hour. Let us call it $500. That means every minute of their time is worth $8.33.

Now think about what happens when that attorney picks up the phone to follow up with a prospect who said "let me think about it" last week:

- Tomorrow, tries again. Gets through. 10-minute conversation that goes nowhere because the prospect is not ready: **$83.30 wasted**

## What AI Follow-Up Looks Like for a Law Firm

Here is a real workflow that runs automatically, without a single human lifting a finger:

**Day 0 (Lead comes in):**
- AI calls, texts, and emails within 5 seconds
- If connected: full intake, qualification, consultation booked
- If not connected: voicemail + text + email sent automatically

**Day 1:**
- AI sends a text: "Hi [Name], this is [Firm Name]. We wanted to follow up on your inquiry about your [case type]. Do you have a few minutes to chat today?"
- If they respond, AI engages in conversation
- If no response, AI notes it and moves to next step

**Day 3:**
- AI calls at a different time of day than the original attempt
- Leaves a warm, professional voicemail referencing the original inquiry
- Sends a follow-up email with firm credentials and case-relevant information

**Day 7:**
- AI sends a text with a value-add: "We put together some information about [relevant legal topic] that might be helpful for your situation. Happy to walk you through it when you're ready."

**Day 14:**
- AI calls again. "Hi [Name], just wanted to make sure you were able to get the help you need. We're still here if you'd like to discuss your case."

**Day 21, 30, 45, 60...**
- Follow-up continues at decreasing frequency, always professional, always referencing the original inquiry and any previous conversations

This happens for **every single lead**, simultaneously, around the clock. An AI system can follow up with 500 leads per day without breaking a sweat. Your intake coordinator can maybe handle 20-30 if that is their only job.
