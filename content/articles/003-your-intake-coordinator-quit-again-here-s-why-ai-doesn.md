## Article 27: Your Intake Coordinator Quit. Again. Here's Why AI Doesn't.

Let me guess: you've gone through three intake coordinators in the last 18 months. Maybe four. The first one was great for about six weeks, then started showing up late. The second one couldn't handle the call volume. The third one left for a firm that pays $2 more an hour.

And every time one quits, you lose a month of momentum, a stack of leads, and whatever institutional knowledge was living in their head instead of your CRM.

### The Intake Coordinator Problem Nobody Talks About

Law firm intake is one of the worst jobs in professional services. Think about it. You're answering phones all day from people having the worst day of their life. Car accident victims. People getting divorced. Business owners being sued. Families dealing with a death.

It's emotionally draining, the pay is mediocre, the hours are long, and the second a better opportunity comes along, they're gone.

And when they leave? They take everything with them. The rapport they built with pending leads. The follow-up schedule they kept in their head (because nobody actually uses the CRM properly). The knowledge of which leads are hot and which are cold.

### The Real Cost of Turnover

Here's what intake coordinator turnover actually costs your firm:

**Direct costs:** Recruiting, hiring, training. Figure $3,000-$5,000 minimum, plus 4-6 weeks of reduced productivity while the new person learns your systems.

**Lost leads:** During the transition, calls get missed. Follow-ups stop. Warm leads go cold. If your firm generates 30 leads a week and you lose even two weeks of proper intake, that's 60 leads that got subpar treatment. At a $3,000 average case value, that's $180,000 in potential revenue — gone.

**Institutional knowledge loss:** The outgoing coordinator knew that Mr. Johnson prefers calls before 9am. That Mrs. Garcia needs a Spanish-speaking attorney. That the Thompson case needs a callback on the 15th. All of that walks out the door.

### AI Doesn't Quit. AI Doesn't Call in Sick. AI Doesn't Have a Bad Day.

An AI intake system works 24/7/365. It doesn't need health insurance. It doesn't need PTO. It doesn't leave for a competitor. And it never, ever forgets to follow up.

Every conversation is logged. Every lead gets the same professional treatment whether it's the first call of the day or the 500th. Every follow-up happens on schedule. Every piece of information is captured in your CRM automatically.

When your AI system talks to a potential client at 11pm on a Saturday — and it will, because that's when people Google "car accident lawyer" — it gathers the case details, qualifies the lead, and either schedules a consultation or warm-transfers to your on-call attorney.

### But What About the Human Touch?

This is the objection I hear from every managing partner. "Our clients need a human touch."

Absolutely. And they'll get one — from your attorneys. The ones billing at $300-$500 an hour. The ones who went to law school to practice law, not to make follow-up calls.

AI handles the 80% grunt work: initial intake, qualification, scheduling, reminders, follow-ups, information gathering. Your humans handle the 20% that actually requires a law degree: consultations, case evaluation, relationship building, and closing.

That's not less human touch. That's more human touch, directed where it matters.

### The Numbers

A good intake coordinator handles maybe 40-50 calls a day and follows up with 10-15 existing leads. That's it. That's their capacity.

An AI system handles unlimited simultaneous conversations across phone, text, and email. It follows up with every lead in your pipeline, every single day, without exception.

One doesn't replace the other entirely — but AI handling first contact and follow-ups means your human team focuses exclusively on high-value conversations with qualified, pre-screened leads.
