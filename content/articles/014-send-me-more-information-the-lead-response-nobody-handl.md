## Article 38: "Send Me More Information" — The Lead Response Nobody Handles (Until Now)

You know the phrase. You've heard it a thousand times. A potential client calls, your intake coordinator has a decent conversation, and right before the close: "Can you just send me some more information? I want to think about it."

Your intake coordinator says "Sure!" Sends a generic email with your firm's brochure. And then... nothing. That lead is gone. Maybe forever.

### Why "Send Me More Information" Is Actually a Buying Signal

Most intake teams treat "send me more information" as a soft rejection. The lead doesn't want to commit. They want to get off the phone. They're not serious.

Wrong. "Send me more information" is a buying signal wrapped in hesitation. What the prospect is really saying is:

- "I'm interested but not ready to make a decision this second"
- "I want to review something before I commit my time to a consultation"
- "I need to justify this to my spouse/partner before I call back"
- "I'm comparing firms and want something tangible to compare"

This is not a dead lead. This is a lead that needs nurturing — and 90% of law firms completely fail at it.

### What Usually Happens

1. Intake coordinator sends a generic email with firm bio, practice areas, and maybe a fee schedule
2. No follow-up. Lead gets filed under "sent info" and forgotten
3. 3 days later, the prospect has signed with whatever firm actually followed up

Or worse: intake coordinator tells the attorney about the lead, attorney says "they'll call back if they're serious," and the lead goes into a black hole.

### What Should Happen

When a prospect says "send me more information," AI kicks into a targeted nurture sequence:

**Immediate (within 5 minutes):**
- Text message: "Hi [name], this is [firm]. Here's the information you requested about [specific case type]. Let me know if you have any questions." + Link to a case-specific landing page (not a generic brochure)
- Email: More detailed information about their specific situation, relevant case results (anonymized), and a clear CTA to book a free consultation

**24 hours later:**
- Text: "Hi [name], just checking — did you get a chance to review the information we sent? Happy to answer any questions about your [case type] situation."

**48 hours later:**
- Phone call from AI: "Hi [name], this is [firm name] following up on the information we sent over. I wanted to see if you had any questions about [specific aspect of their case]. Attorney [name] has some availability this week for a free case evaluation."

**5 days later:**
- Text with a specific value prop: "Just wanted to share — we recently helped a client in a situation very similar to yours. The consultation is free and there's no obligation. Would you like to schedule a quick 15-minute call?"

**10 days later:**
- Final touchpoint with urgency (if applicable): "Quick reminder that [relevant deadline — statute of limitations, filing deadline, etc.] may apply to your situation. Worth getting a free evaluation before time runs out."

### Why This Sequence Works

Each touchpoint does something different:

- **The text** catches them where they actually communicate (97% text open rate vs. 20% email)
- **The email** gives them something to review and share with their spouse
- **The phone call** re-establishes human connection (even from AI, it feels personal)
- **The value-specific follow-ups** address the real objection (fear of wasting time/money)
- **The urgency message** gives them a reason to act now

### The Numbers

Firms that implement this kind of "information requested" sequence convert 25-35% of "send me more info" leads into consultations. Firms that send a single email and forget about it convert 3-5%.
