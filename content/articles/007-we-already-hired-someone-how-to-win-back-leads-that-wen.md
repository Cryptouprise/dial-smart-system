## Article 31: "We Already Hired Someone" — How to Win Back Leads That Went Cold

Every law firm has heard it. You call a lead back — maybe two days late, maybe two weeks late — and they hit you with: "Oh, we already hired an attorney."

It stings. But here's what should really sting: that person wanted to hire YOU. They reached out to YOUR firm. They filled out YOUR form or called YOUR number. And by the time you got around to following up, they'd moved on.

### Why Leads Go Cold (It's Your Fault)

Let's be brutally honest here. Leads don't go cold because they changed their mind about needing a lawyer. They go cold because:

- You took too long to respond (even 30 minutes is too long)
- You called once, they didn't answer, and nobody called again
- Your intake coordinator was busy with other leads
- It was after hours and nobody was available
- The follow-up fell through the cracks

The prospect didn't reject you. You rejected them — by not showing up.

### The "Already Hired Someone" Isn't Always Final

Here's something most firms don't realize: when someone says "I already hired someone," that doesn't mean they're happy about it. It means they needed help, nobody at your firm was available, so they went with whoever picked up the phone.

Within the first 48-72 hours of hiring an attorney, buyers remorse is real. They haven't signed anything binding (in most cases). They're still in evaluation mode. And if a better option presents itself — say, a professional AI-assisted follow-up from your firm that addresses their specific situation — they'll consider switching.

### The Win-Back Sequence

AI doesn't hear "we already hired someone" and give up. It responds intelligently:

**Initial response:** "Totally understand. I hope everything works out with your case. Just in case, I'd like to send you a quick guide on [relevant topic] — no strings attached. Can I text that to you?"

**3 days later:** "Just checking in — how's everything going with your case? If you ever need a second opinion, Attorney [name] is always available for a free review."

**2 weeks later:** "Hi [name], it's been a couple weeks since we last spoke. We've helped a lot of people in similar situations to yours. If anything changes or you'd like a second perspective, we're here."

**30 days later:** A final touchpoint. Friendly, not pushy.

This sequence recovers 5-10% of "already hired" leads. These are people who had a bad experience with their first attorney, discovered hidden fees, or realized they made a hasty decision.

### Prevention Is Better Than Recovery

The best way to handle "we already hired someone" is to never hear it in the first place. That means:

**Speed to lead:** Respond in 5 seconds, not 5 hours. The first firm to call wins 78% of the time.

**Multi-channel outreach:** Don't just call. Call, text, AND email simultaneously. Meet the lead wherever they are.

**24/7 availability:** Leads don't come in on a 9-to-5 schedule. Your intake shouldn't either.

**Persistent follow-up:** If they don't answer the first call, don't just leave a voicemail and move on. AI follows up 8, 10, 12 times across multiple channels until there's a real conversation.
