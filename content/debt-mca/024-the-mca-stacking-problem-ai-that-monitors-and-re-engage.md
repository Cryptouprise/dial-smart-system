# Article 24: The MCA Stacking Problem: AI That Monitors and Re-Engages

## Stacking Is the MCA Industry's Billion-Dollar Headache

Every MCA company deals with stacking. A merchant takes an advance from you, then takes another from a competitor, then another. Suddenly your daily ACH is competing with two other funders for the same revenue, default risk skyrockets, and your portfolio takes a hit.

But here's the flip side that nobody talks about: **stacking is also an opportunity.**

A merchant who's stacking is a merchant who needs more money than you gave them. They're actively in the market. They've demonstrated willingness to take on advances. And they already have a relationship with you.

The question isn't "how do we prevent stacking?" It's "how do we capture the next advance before someone else does?"

## The Monitoring Play

AI can monitor your funded portfolio for stacking signals:

**Daily ACH failures or NSFs.** When a merchant starts bouncing ACH debits, something changed. Either revenue dropped or another funder is pulling from the same account.

**Payment pattern changes.** If a merchant who's been paying smoothly suddenly requests a payment modification or restructure, they might have taken on additional advances.

**Revenue drops.** If you have bank statement access or monitoring, declining monthly deposits signal either a business problem or stacking.

**Time-based triggers.** Merchants who've been on their advance for 50%+ of the term are statistically most likely to seek additional funding. They've gotten comfortable with the payments and want to reload.

AI monitors all of these signals across your entire portfolio. Not just the ones your account managers remember to check. ALL of them.

## The Re-Engagement Sequence

When AI detects a stacking signal or a renewal opportunity, it kicks off a re-engagement sequence:

**For potential stackers:**
*"Hey [Name], just checking in on how business is going. I noticed [subtle signal]. If you're thinking about additional working capital, I'd love to put an offer together before you look elsewhere. We can often offer better terms for existing customers."*

**For renewal-ready merchants:**
*"[Name], you're about [X]% through your current advance and it looks like things are going well. A lot of our merchants at this point like to discuss a renewal to access more capital. Want me to run some numbers?"*

**For at-risk merchants:**
*"Hey [Name], I noticed your payments have been a little tight lately. No judgment -- it happens to every business. I want to make sure you're set up for success. Can we talk about options?"*
